# CSNSA

A web-based Human Resources and Attendance Management system for teams and organizations. It gives HR staff, managers, and administrators one place to manage employees, track working hours, plan shifts, and handle absences.

## What is it for?

Many workplaces struggle to keep attendance, schedules, and employee information in one reliable place. Spreadsheets and separate tools make it hard to know who is working, who is away, and how many hours each person has done.

HR teams, managers, and administrators face this problem every day. They need accurate records for payroll, compliance, and day-to-day planning — but scattered data leads to mistakes, delays, and wasted time.

CSNSA solves this by bringing employee records, time tracking, shift planning, absences, and reporting together in a single admin panel. It is especially useful for workplaces that use physical time clocks or biometric devices, because clock-in and clock-out data can be collected automatically.

## What does it do?

- View a live dashboard showing who is working, on break, or away
- Add, edit, archive, and export employee records with photos and work details
- Organize staff into teams and departments
- Record and review clock-in and clock-out times for each employee
- Connect biometric time clocks so punches from physical devices are saved automatically
- Create work shifts and build monthly schedules, including bulk import
- Manage absence requests, justifications, and supporting documents
- Track worked hours, overtime, and hour balances over time
- Generate hour and absence reports for payroll and review
- Control who can log in and what each user is allowed to view or edit
- Switch between light and dark themes for comfortable daily use

## How does it work?

1. An administrator logs in to the admin panel. On a new setup, the first registered user becomes the administrator; after that, only authorized users can be added.
2. Employee records, teams, shifts, and schedules are created and stored in the database.
3. Employees clock in and out through connected time clocks (or entries are managed directly in the panel), and each punch is saved to their profile.
4. Managers review attendance on the dashboard, handle absence requests, and use the hour bank and reports to check totals and overtime.
5. The result is a clear, up-to-date picture of who worked, when they worked, and how hours balance across the team.
